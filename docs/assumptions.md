# Assumptions

Every analytical model simplifies reality. The Driver Talent Evaluation Framework is built on the following assumptions.

---

# 1. Teammates Drive Comparable Machinery

The framework assumes that teammates compete in equivalent cars during a race weekend.

This assumption allows teammate comparisons to serve as a proxy for removing the influence of car performance.

While minor setup differences may exist, the overall machinery is considered sufficiently similar for analytical purposes.

---

# 2. Qualifying Position Represents One-Lap Pace

Qualifying position is used as an indicator of raw qualifying performance.

The model assumes that consistently qualifying ahead of a teammate reflects stronger one-lap pace.

---

# 3. Finishing Position Represents Race Performance

Race performance is evaluated using finishing positions relative to teammates.

The model assumes that, after removing races affected by mechanical failures, finishing ahead of a teammate generally indicates superior race execution.

---

# 4. Mechanical Failures Should Not Influence Talent Evaluation

Mechanical retirements and reliability issues do not reflect driver ability.

Therefore, races affected by mechanical or external failures are excluded from teammate race comparisons.

---

# 5. Modern Era Drivers Are Comparable

The analysis is limited to the 2000–2024 seasons.

This assumes that drivers competing within this period raced under broadly comparable regulations and competitive environments.

Cross-era comparisons are intentionally avoided.

---

# 6. Every Valid Teammate Comparison Has Equal Weight

Each valid teammate comparison contributes equally to the analysis.

The model does not assign additional importance based on:

* Championship standings
* Race prestige
* Weather conditions
* Team orders

---

# 7. Performance Metrics Capture Driver Ability

The framework assumes that the selected KPIs collectively represent driver performance.

These include:

* Qualifying Win Rate
* Average Qualifying Gap
* Race Win Rate
* Average Finish Gap

No single metric is considered sufficient on its own.

---

# 8. Talent and Confidence Measure Different Concepts

Talent Index estimates driver performance.

Confidence Score estimates the reliability of that estimate based on the amount of available teammate comparison data.

The two metrics are intentionally kept independent.

---

# 9. Standard Deviation Represents Performance Consistency

Standard deviation is used as a descriptive measure of performance variability.

Lower standard deviation indicates more repeatable performances relative to teammates.

Consistency is reported separately and is not included in the Talent Index calculation.

---

# Summary

These assumptions simplify the complex nature of Formula 1 competition while allowing the framework to produce an interpretable, teammate-relative estimate of driver talent.
